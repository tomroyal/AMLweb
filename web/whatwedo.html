What We Do

We publish award-winning magazines on tablet devices, smartphones and in print, help other creators of print products to make the transition to bespoke, re-imagined digital products, and develop software tools for publishing on touchscreen and mobile devices.

Our own publications include the British Journal of Photography and Popular Science UK, while our recent projects include the iPad edition of 125 Magazine.

We also create bespoke interactive advertising for tablets and smartphones, and we are the official UK representative of the Mag+ digital publishing system.

If you're interested in transforming a magazine, brochure, advert, catalogue or report into an immersive experience on the iPad, iPhone or any other touchscreen device, please visit our creative studio website or get in touch.

British Journal of Photography

First published in 1854, the British Journal of Photography (BJP) is the oldest continually published photography magazine in the world. In 2011, the team behind Apptitude Media re-imagined it into an award winning iPad magazine.

The BJP was recently awarded 'International Photography Magazine of the Year' at the prestigious Lucie Awards held in Los Angeles, while the Quarterly iPad edition won 'Best Use of Mobile' at the 2012 British Media Awards.

In 2012 the team launched a new app, BJP+, bringing the full monthly magazine to the iPad alongside weekly issues for the iPhone.

Find the BJP online, and in the App Store.

Popular Science UK

First published in 1872, Popular Science is one of the world's leading science magazines, currently published in New York by Bonnier Corporation. An interactive iPad edition was launched on the same day as the iPad itself, and gained notable praise from Steve Jobs, who described it in a keynote as 'king of the hill' among iPad magazines.

In 2012 the team behind Apptitude Media worked with Bonnier to bring an exclusive new edition of the magazine to the UK, complete with new UK exclusive product news, columns and feature articles.

Find Popular Science UK online, and in the App Store.
